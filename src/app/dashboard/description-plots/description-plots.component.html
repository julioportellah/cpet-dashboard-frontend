The SHAP summary plot does not represent the odds of having a condition or not but rather the influence of being in that category. Thus, if your feature has a high shapley value, the feature has a possitive relationship with the condition predicted.

The feature importance is sorted from top to bottom. For this model, we can see that the Difference betwee the actual peak VO2 and the expected peak VO2 is the most important feature for the model. This can be seen in some research projects done in the past.

The color bar on the right tell us if a dot has a high actual value, the blue ones tell that the dot had a low actual value. Each dot represent each data sample where the experiment was run. The sum of all the dots generates the pattern. With more datapoints the better pattern.

Finally, there is a grey line that divides the summary plot. The values on the left indicates negative shapley values while the ones in the right mean high shapley values. Finally, a full interpretation in this case will mean that if you have red dots on the left and blue dots on the right, your feature when it has a high value reduces the odds of having a cardiac limitation. If your feature has a low value, it actually increases the odds of having a cardiac limitation. Thus, the difference between the actual vs the expected peak VO2 has an inverse relationship with the cardiac limitation.

The dependency plot behaves more like a regular curve. Although it doesn't show a curve, it may show a pattern that is modeled by the algorithm. The Y-axis represents the shapley values, thus the influence on having a cardiac limitation or not. The higher the value the higher the chances.
